Severe storms move through I-95 corridor from Pennsylvania to Carolinas with hail, gusty winds

A cold front sweeping into the East brought the threat of severe weather from Philadelphia and Washington D.C. south into the Carolina beaches on Saturday. Damaging wind gusts and large hail were the greatest risk, but there were a few Tornado Warnings.

Severe Thunderstorm Watch includes North Carolina, Virginia, Maryland and Delaware

Storms on Saturday have the potential to produce hail and damaging winds along Intestate 95 from the South Carolina-North Carolina border through Maryland.

The final day of a multiday severe event brought the threat of damaging winds and hail to millions from eastern Pennsylvania and New Jersey southward to the Carolinas.  

Severe Thunderstorm Watches stretched hundreds of miles on Saturday afternoon and local National Weather Service offices even issued a handful of Tornado Warnings for the stronger storms.

The cold front responsible for the storms kicked off with severe weather over the Plains on Tuesday. Less than 24 hours later, a slug of moisture helped storms develop from the Midwest to Texas and were responsible for killing at least three people in Cole, Oklahoma. Several tornadoes struck south and east of the Oklahoma City metro Wednesday night.

The increased threat of storms on Saturday diminished once the frontal boundary moved offshore, leaving behind a much clearer and more comfortable Sunday.

Tornado causes damage in Cole, Oklahoma

A tornado caused damage in Cole, Oklahoma, as an outbreak erupted on Wednesday. 

Storms were reported from Florida through Pennsylvania with sporadic damage that primarily involved falling trees.

Poolesville, Maryland, and communities in eastern Pennsylvania appeared to be the hardest hit. Trees were reported down in the towns of Palmyra and Hershey and there were extensive power outages in the state.

Immediately after the storms, PowerOutage.us reported around 35,000 outages in Pennsylvania with over 10,000 in Maryland.
