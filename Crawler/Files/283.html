Hail coats ground in Texas town as stormy weekend in Lone Star State begins

About 1-2 inches of rain is likely in Austin and San Antonio on Sunday, with 2-3 inches likely around Corpus Christi.

By Aaron Barker , Scott Sistek Source FOX Weather

Hail coats ground in Ballinger, TX

A significant hailstorm impacted central Texas on Saturday afternoon.

CORPUS CHRISTI, Texas – It's looking like an indoor kind of weekend across parts of central and South Texas as a developing area of low pressure will bring heavy rains and a threat of severe thunderstorms to the region. 

A supercell thunderstorm dropped lots of hail on a rural Texas town just northeast of San Angelo on Saturday afternoon.

Images and videos shared with FOX Weather from residents of Ballinger in Runnels County showed dime- to quarter-size hail covering the ground. Fog caused by the cold hailstones could also be seen rising from the ground.

According to the National Weather Service, a storm spotter reported baseball- to tea-cup-size hail in Ballinger.

Saturday's severe thunderstorm risk centered on Central Texas

Severe storms fired in parts of Central Texas on Saturday, as a Severe Thunderstorm Watch was issued for the region until Saturday night. Storms were expected to continue into the evening before weakening after sunset.

The greatest threat from any severe thunderstorm Saturday is large hail, with a bull's-eye of higher risk of at least 2-inch diameter hail near the San Angelo area. Damaging wind gusts of over 60 mph are possible in any of these thunderstorms, while a few isolated tornadoes possible as well.

San Antonio and Austin could also see some storms, but the risk is a bit lower than closer to San Angelo.

Flash flooding risk increases across South Texas on Sunday

Steady rain expands to cover just about all of central and eastern Texas on Sunday, with a rainy day on tap for the Dallas-Fort Worth metroplex and Houston. 

Heavier rains are forecast amid a building severe thunderstorm threat for about 5 million people in South Texas, including San Antonio, Brownsville, and Corpus Christi. Any severe thunderstorms that develop in this area carry a risk of damaging wind gusts over 60 mph and large hail. There is even a low chance of a few tornadoes in the region.  


 

About 1-2 inches of rain is likely in Austin and San Antonio on Sunday, with 2-3 inches likely around Corpus Christi.

The persistent rain will not only wash out many outdoor plans, but with recent rains and saturated soils, the storm could also bring enough rainfall to trigger flash flooding.

"While this area hasn't been quite as wet as areas north towards San Antonio, soil moisture is above average for this time of year from recent rains," wrote forecasters with NOAA's Weather Prediction Center. "And so the flash flooding risk, particularly in metro Corpus Christi is notable."
